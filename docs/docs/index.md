# Student Dropout Research

## Description

This is a proactive tool designed to identify and support at-risk students at Hogeschool Utrecht. Leveraging predictive analytics on enrollment data, the tool aims to detect potential dropouts early in their academic journey. This allows for timely interventions that are tailored to individual needs, enhancing student success and promoting equity in educational outcomes.

## Project Plan for Early Warning Tool Design: "No fairness, without awareness"

## Background

Within the literature and government policy surrounding student success and equal opportunities, there is broad consensus that to organize equal outcomes between students, unequal treatment is necessary (see, for example, Equal Opportunities Alliance, Ministry of Education, Culture and Science 2024). Hogeschool Utrecht (HU) has set itself the goal of contributing to equal opportunities in the region, among other things through its own education. At the same time, both the literature (Elffers, 2018; Wolff, 2013) and HU's own data (HU Data & Analytics Portal, 2024) show that some student groups are disproportionately vulnerable to lack of student success. This problem has existed for some time, but despite a wide range of efforts within HU, it proves difficult for individual programs to organize education in such a way that not only equal opportunities but also equal outcomes arise between student groups. The focus on equal outcomes is important and forms the difference between "equality" and "equity" (Bakker, 2024).

A key reason for the persistence of this issue is that programs relatively need a lot of time to find out which students would benefit from extra or customized guidance. By the time vulnerable students come into view of a student counselor or dean, they often have already accrued delays that are difficult to recover from, as the first 100 days are crucial for organizing a successful student experience (Kappe, 2017). This situation is serious because literature shows that, for example, students who drop out not only lose time and money, but often also motivation and self-confidence (Vossensteyn et al., 2015). Meanwhile, dropout rates within HU are higher than the national average, making it urgent to intervene in this situation.

## Project Objective

The aim of this project is to design a prototype that identifies vulnerable students based on enrollment data at a stage where customized guidance can still lead to them experiencing student success at HU. In this way, the project contributes to the creation of leading indicators (organizing customized guidance) instead of lagging indicators (dropout and study delays).

## Scope

This project involves the design and development of a prototype to organize early identification of full-time students who are vulnerable to dropout and lack of student success based on Studielink enrollment data, including the technical and ethical dilemmas that such a tool brings. Guidance issues are outside the scope of this project.

In a separate project (Discover Me), work is currently being done on a pre-bachelor aimed at strengthening vulnerable students along the axes of qualification, socialization, and subjectification (Biesta, 2015). This trajectory lies outside the scope of this project, but regular coordination is maintained.

In a subsequent phase, the methodology can be explored on how a tool focused on early identification could specifically contribute to recognizing students who would benefit from 1) participation in a pre-bachelor trajectory or 2) enhanced guidance during the first 100 days. Students would then be informed about the possibilities for extra guidance within HU at the front of their student experience at HU based on voluntariness.

## Project Structure

The project leader is responsible for the direction, content, and execution of this project in coordination with the MT of the FCA service. The project leader reports to the manager of Data & Analytics. The project leader performs activities in collaboration with a project group, consisting of one or two data engineers, a legal advisor, and an educational advisor.

**Project Leader**: Responsible for general project management and execution.

**Data Engineer/Scientist(s)**: Responsible for the technical development of the tool.

**Legal Advisor**: Responsible for ensuring compliance with laws and regulations.

**Ethics Committee**: Ensures ethical considerations and moral deliberation.
